An assembled, ready-to-run open-source robot arm

The SO-101 is an open-source desktop robot arm with 5 DOF and a parallel gripper, built on the open SO-ARM100/SO-101 design and compatible with Hugging Face LeRobot. Hashtag Robotics ships it leader + follower together, assembled and tested, so you skip kit assembly and start running code on day one. It is on free pre-order now, with no payment collected.

Most open-source arms reach you as a kit: a bag of parts, printed components and an assembly guide that can take days to work through. We took a different path. Every SO-101 leaves us fully built, calibrated and tested, so the time you would have spent assembling goes straight into your actual robotics and AI work.

The platform pairs an operator-guided leader arm with a task-performing follower arm. You move the leader by hand and the follower mirrors it one to one, which is exactly the setup teleoperation, data collection and imitation learning workflows are built around.

Frequently asked

Common questions.

What is the SO-101?

The SO-101 is an open-source desktop robot arm with 5 degrees of freedom and a parallel gripper, compatible with the Hugging Face LeRobot ecosystem. It comes as two arms, a leader and a follower, and is designed for teleoperation, data collection and imitation learning.

Does the robot arm ship assembled or as a kit?

It ships assembled and tested. There is no part-by-part assembly; you take it out of the box, plug it in and start using it.

Where can I buy an open-source robot arm?

Hashtag Robotics offers the SO-101 on pre-order. Fill in the pre-order form to reserve your place in line; when your production slot opens we reach out to you with the price and delivery date.

What is the SO-101 used for?

It is used for robotics and AI education, university and research labs, prototyping, teleoperation and imitation learning projects.

What software does it run?

It runs on Hugging Face's open-source LeRobot library. It is programmed in Python and supports teleoperation, data collection and policy training.

What are the leader and follower arms?

The follower is the arm that performs the tasks. The leader is the operator-guided controller arm; it mirrors its motion onto the follower one to one, enabling teleoperation and data collection. The SO-101 pre-order includes both together.
